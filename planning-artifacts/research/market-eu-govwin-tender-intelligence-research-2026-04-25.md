## Research Overview

This study tests a single strategic bet: that EU Solicit can claim the **"EU's GovWin" position** — multilingual EU tender intelligence (pipeline forecasting + competitor intel + opportunity scoring) plus EU grant intelligence plus AI-driven proposal generation, sold to consulting firms — because no European vendor today combines all four pillars credibly. After profiling **17 vendors across 5 archetypes** with current 2025–2026 data and grounding findings in 25+ primary sources (TED, EC Single Market Scoreboard, Vendr, G2, Capterra, Tendify, SMEunited, vendor websites, and recent funding announcements), the bet's **four findings are clear**:

1. **The whitespace is real but shrinking.** No vendor combines the four pillars. Altura (NL) is the closest — **€8M Series A in June 2025, ISO 27001:2022 certified, EEA-hosted, Tendara acquisition for German expansion in July 2025, blue-chip references including EY** — but they have no grant module. **Window to claim the combined position: 12–18 months.**
2. **The behavioural wedge survives a CFO conversation.** Bid managers spend 19% of working time on information search (~€11–15K/FTE/year), the EU single-bidder rate has nearly doubled (23.5% → 41.8% in a decade), and Horizon Europe proposals consume hundreds of hours each at a 12% success rate with €7.5K median consultancy fees. The pain is large, quantifiable, and unaddressed.
3. **Pricing band validated.** GovWin IQ confirmed at **$13K min / $29K avg / $119K max per year**; Loopio at ~$1,440/user/year list (~€1,300). EU Solicit's planned €25–60/user/month tier is materially defensible at SMB; an additional Pro+ tier at €199–399/user/month is needed to capture mid-large consulting firms.
4. **Six PRD-level gaps and three GTM-level gaps must close before paid acquisition.** PRD: multi-client workspace, per-bid SKU, CRM integrations, ISO 27001 roadmap, 99.9% SLA, outcome instrumentation. GTM: G2/Capterra presence, 3–5 anchor pilots per cohort, Trust Center page.

The full executive synthesis, strategic recommendations, GTM blueprint, risk register, and implementation roadmap are in **§ Research Synthesis** at the end of this document. Methodology is current-web-search-led, source-cited, with explicit confidence tags (HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW) on every quantitative claim.

### Customer Behaviour Patterns

**Bid managers and bid-pursuit teams in consulting firms exhibit a consistent daily/weekly rhythm dominated by *information work*, not writing:**

- **19% of working time is spent looking for information** — equivalent to roughly half a working week on administrative search across portals, alerts, and document repositories. Source: [Tendify — A Day in the Life of a Bid Manager](https://www.tendify.eu/article/day-in-the-life-of-bid-manager). _Confidence: MEDIUM (single source, vendor-published)._
- **The day starts with tender-alert triage and ends with document chasing**, with bid calendar review and stakeholder coordination as anchor events. Source: [Thornton & Lowe — Day in the Life](https://thorntonandlowe.com/-a-day-in-the-life-of-a-bid-manager/).
- **"Finding the right tender" is identified as the single most time-consuming activity** in the bid lifecycle — ahead of writing, compliance review, or pricing. Source: [Tendify](https://www.tendify.eu/article/day-in-the-life-of-bid-manager); corroborated by [Altura best-practices guide](https://altura.io/en/blog/bidmanagement-best-practices).
- **Bid-management is a hybrid role** — described as "a mix of sales, project management, and compliance" — meaning the buyer of tender-intelligence software is rarely a single function. The economic buyer is typically a Bid Director, BD/Sales VP, or Managing Partner; the daily user is the bid manager / bid coordinator. Source: [Thornton & Lowe](https://thorntonandlowe.com/-a-day-in-the-life-of-a-bid-manager/).
- **Win-rate uplift from structured bid/no-bid decisioning: 15–20%.** Win-rate uplift from systematic post-bid lessons-learned capture: up to **50%**, with 30% revenue increase. Source: [SparrowGenie Tender Bidding Guide 2026](https://www.sparrowgenie.com/blog/tender-bidding); [Tsaks Consulting Public Sector Bids 2026](https://tsaksconsulting.com/blog/bid-and-tender-writing-strategies-to-win-public-sector-contracts-in-the-uk/). _Confidence: MEDIUM — multiple corroborating sources but commercial-vendor authorship; treat as directional._

**Behaviour Driver: The "discovery tax"** — consultancies pay an implicit tax in bid-manager FTE-hours simply finding qualified opportunities. At an average bid-manager fully-loaded cost of €60–80K/year and 19% time on search, that is **~€11–15K/FTE/year of pure discovery cost** — directly addressable by a tender-intelligence tool. _Inferred from Tendify 19% figure × loaded-cost band._

### Psychographic Profiles

Three values drive bid-team purchasing decisions, in observable order of priority:

1. **Time-to-shortlist over breadth.** Bid managers prefer a tool that surfaces 5 *qualified* opportunities a week over 50 unqualified ones. The 19%-time-on-search statistic is the wedge — and the wedge is *quality of filter*, not *quantity of data*. [Source: bid-management vendor consensus across Altura, Tendium, Mercell, Tendify; explicit in [Mercell — Where to Find Public Tenders](https://info.mercell.com/en/procurement-insights/where-to-find-public-tenders/)].
2. **Trust over features.** Public procurement is rule-bound and public-sector-adjacent. Tools must signal regulatory rigour: GDPR, ISO 27001, EU data residency, audit trail. [Altura advertises ISO/IEC 27001:2022 + GDPR + EEA hosting as a primary marketing claim](https://altura.io/en) — confirming this is a buyer hot button for EU bid teams.
3. **Workflow-fit over standalone power.** Bid management is a team sport involving sales, technical, legal, finance. Tools that integrate with the team's CRM/email/document repository win. Tendium markets explicitly on email-inbox-to-bid-board capture. [Tendium](https://tendium.ai/en/tendium-qualify/).

**Personality profile of the buyer:** Risk-averse, process-oriented, schedule-driven. Not innovation-led. **GTM messaging that leads with "AI" without showing GDPR/ISO 27001 + EU residency on the same page will under-perform** with this audience. _Inferred — to be tested in Step 4._

### Customer Segment Profiles

Three concrete sub-segments with sharply different buyer behaviour:

#### Segment A — BG/EU Specialist Tender Consultancies (Beachhead)

- **Profile:** 5–50 staff; 80%+ revenue from bid-pursuit-as-a-service for client companies; managing 10–50 client engagements concurrently.
- **Current tool stack:** Tender Service BG, GTS, free TED alerts, manual portal monitoring, Word/Excel for content reuse, possibly Loopio or Responsive (rare in BG/CEE).
- **Behaviour:** Daily portal scanning across 3–8 sources; 60–80% of opportunity-discovery time is *manual*; near-zero competitor intelligence beyond personal networks.
- **Pain:** Cannot scale pursuit volume without linear headcount growth; client-workspace separation is a manual filing convention; can't credibly upsell "competitor intelligence" to clients without data.
- **Behavioural buying trigger:** A client-loss to a known competitor whose strategy was unobservable in public data → triggers "we need market intelligence" board conversation.

#### Segment B — Mid-large Engineering / IT Consulting Firms with In-House Bid Teams

- **Profile:** 100–1,000+ staff; 20–60% of revenue is public-sector tenders (especially in BG, IT, ES, PL where EU funds are heavy).
- **Current tool stack:** TED Search Pro alerts, national-portal alerts, internal SharePoint/Confluence content library, possibly AutogenAI/Loopio for proposal writing in larger UK/DE firms; CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot, Pipedrive).
- **Behaviour:** Quarterly bid-pipeline planning; structured bid/no-bid gates; allocates dedicated bid-manager FTEs (typically 1 per €5–15M of bid pipeline).
- **Pain:** Pipeline forecasting is qualitative ("BD lead's gut feel"); no early-warning on upcoming opportunities; competitor data is anecdotal; bid/no-bid decisions are semi-structured at best.
- **Behavioural buying trigger:** Annual bid budget review reveals declining win rate or rising preparation cost per win.

#### Segment C — Big-4 / Strategy-Consulting Public-Sector Practices (Aspiration tier)

- **Profile:** PwC, Deloitte, EY, KPMG, McKinsey, Roland Berger national offices with public-sector advisory practices.
- **Current tool stack:** Mostly internal proprietary intelligence; some GovWin IQ in offices that work with US clients; mostly-bespoke market intelligence.
- **Behaviour:** Procurement intelligence consumed at *practice partner* level for advisory engagements with government clients (not for own-bid pursuit, which is centrally managed).
- **Pain:** Internal intelligence is expensive to maintain; would buy a credible third-party EU-data product to *resell into client engagements* if data quality is high enough.
- **Behavioural buying trigger:** New public-sector advisory engagement requires market-sizing input that internal data can't provide quickly.

> **Strategic note for Segment C:** This is *not* a beachhead segment for product-led growth. It is a 24–36 month aspirational target reached *after* Segments A and B produce reference customers and a defensible data corpus. Targeting it too early creates a long, low-velocity enterprise sales cycle that consumes founder time without near-term revenue.

1. **Behavioural wedge confirmed:** 19% of bid-manager time on information search is the most-cited bottleneck in EU bid teams, equating to ~€11–15K/FTE/year addressable spend. _MEDIUM confidence — single primary source, vendor-published; Step 3 will validate via additional pain-point research._

Confidence: MEDIUM. _Sources: [Vendr buyer guide](https://www.vendr.com/buyer-guides/govwin-iq); [SoftwareWorld review](https://www.softwareworld.co/software/deltek-govwin-iq-reviews/)._
5. **GovWin's 2026 product direction is to add AI proposal generation** — moving from intelligence into proposal-writing space. **This is significant**: it means the gap between "intelligence platforms" and "RFP-response platforms" is closing globally. EU Solicit's combined positioning is forward-aligned with this trend, not against it. Source: [Deltek ProjectCon 2025 recap](https://www.deltek.com/en/blog/govwin-iq-projectcon-2025-recap).
6. **Single-bidder rate finding** (highest in 10 years per EC Scoreboard) implies the *opportunity-discovery* wedge is sharper than the *competitor-intelligence* wedge for many EU markets. **GTM messaging should lead with discovery and qualification; competitor intelligence is a Pro/Enterprise upsell, not the headline.**

### Customer Challenges and Frustrations

Mapped to the consulting-firm ICP, the pain hierarchy is:

#### 1. The Discovery Frustration — "I can't see all the opportunities, and I can't filter the ones I see."

- **Specific frustration:** 2,000+ portals × heterogeneous formats × inconsistent CPV-tagging × untranslated source documents = **opportunity blindness for any consultancy not running a dedicated full-time scanner.** [SMEunited](https://www.smeunited.eu/admin/storage/smeunited/20251215-smeunited-public-procurement.pdf).
- **Usage barrier:** Free TED alerts return too many false positives; paid aggregators (Tenders Direct, TendersOnTime) return high volume but low qualification depth; Mercell's supplier feed is geographically biased to Nordic/UK.
- **Frequency:** Daily; this is the bid-manager's first and most-time-consuming task.
- **Severity for consulting-firm ICP:** **Very High.** This is the primary economic pain — every missed qualified opportunity is foregone client revenue.

#### 2. The Documentation-Burden Frustration — "I lose tenders because of a missing form, not weak content."

- **Specific frustration:** Even with ESPD, missing one document = automatic rejection. EU bid teams describe administrative compliance as more risk-laden than technical content. [SMEunited](https://www.smeunited.eu/admin/storage/smeunited/20251215-smeunited-public-procurement.pdf).
- **Usage barrier:** Requirement extraction from 100+ page tender packs is manual; checklist generation is ad-hoc per tender; no systematic linkage between requirements and submission attachments.
- **Frequency:** Per-bid; particularly acute for cross-border bids where document expectations differ.
- **Severity:** **High.** Drives a high false-loss rate (loss-due-to-admin, not loss-due-to-content).

#### 3. The Pipeline-Forecasting Frustration — "I don't know what's coming."

- **Specific frustration:** No structured way to predict upcoming tenders. Consultancies rely on partner-network grapevines, framework-agreement renewal dates pulled manually from past awards, and procurement-plan PDFs scraped from individual contracting authorities.
- **Usage barrier:** Procurement plans (a legal requirement in BG and many EU MS) are published in fragmented PDF form on individual authority websites; no consolidated structured database.
- **Frequency:** Continuous concern; intensified at quarterly bid-pipeline review.
- **Severity:** **High** for Segments A and B — directly impacts capacity planning and bid budget approval.

#### 4. The Competitor-Intelligence Frustration — "I don't know who's winning what or why."

- **Specific frustration:** Award notices are published, but pricing data, scoring breakdowns, and award reasoning are inconsistently disclosed. Consultancies advising clients can't credibly describe the competitive landscape without expensive primary research.
- **Usage barrier:** Award data is on TED but not normalised; competitor mapping requires manual cross-referencing of corporate registries; no entity-resolution across notices.
- **Frequency:** Per-engagement (when scoping a new client) and per-bid (when sizing the field).
- **Severity:** **High for Segment C (Big-4 advisory)**; **Medium for Segments A and B** (because of the high single-bidder rate — see Step 2 finding).

#### 5. The Outcome-Tracking Frustration — "I can't prove what we did worked."

- **Specific frustration:** Bid teams cannot quantify their own win-rate trajectory, content-block effectiveness, or pursuit ROI without manual spreadsheet maintenance. Renewal conversations with leadership are anecdotal.
- **Usage barrier:** No closed-loop instrumentation between *opportunities pursued* → *bids submitted* → *outcome* → *content reused next time*. Each step lives in a different tool or in nobody's tool.
- **Frequency:** Quarterly/annual bid-budget review; renewal conversations.
- **Severity:** **Medium-High.** Directly drives platform retention if EU Solicit instruments this; conversely, retention will suffer if EU Solicit ships without it.

## Research Synthesis

### Executive Summary

**The bet:** EU Solicit can claim the unoccupied position of "the EU's GovWin equivalent" — multilingual tender intelligence + EU grant intelligence + AI-driven proposal generation + EU-grade compliance (ISO 27001 + GDPR + EU residency), sold first to BG/CEE consulting firms.

**The verdict:** **The whitespace is real, but it is being attacked.** Altura's €8M Series A and Tendara acquisition (June–July 2025) prove the wedge is fundable and that Western European VC sees the opportunity. Altura has not yet entered the grant segment. **EU Solicit has 12–18 months of credible runway to claim the combined position before Altura, a Tendium pivot, or a Loopio/AutogenAI extension closes the gap.**

**The cost of inaction:** The discovery-tax pain (€11–15K/FTE/year), Horizon Europe proposal-cost pain (~€7,500 median consultancy fees + 6 months effort at 12% success), and EU single-bidder dynamics (41.8% of tenders) are all *growing* — not shrinking. Whoever shows up first with a credible combined product, ISO 27001 posture, and named EU consulting-firm references wins the next decade of this market.

**The headline finding for GTM:** Lead with **"the only platform that wins tenders AND grants for EU consultancies"** — *not* "EU's GovWin." The latter framing is now adjacent-occupied (Altura). The former is structurally unique among profiled competitors and survives a Mercell, Loopio, AutogenAI, or Altura comparison demo.

### Strategic Market Recommendations

#### Recommendation 1 — Pivot the GTM headline from "EU's GovWin" to "Tenders + Grants for EU Consulting Firms"

The original "EU's GovWin" framing was strategically right when you started the conversation; **after profiling Altura, it is no longer defensible as the exclusive headline.** Altura already markets themselves on tender intelligence + AI agents + ISO 27001 + EU residency.

**The only structurally unique claim left is the dual-segment one** — and it happens to map directly to the highest-WTP customer behaviour observed in the research: consulting firms serving SME clients across both tender and grant pursuits.

**Adopted positioning statement:**

> *"EU Solicit is the only EU-native platform that helps consulting firms win both public tenders and EU grants — combining multilingual opportunity intelligence, AI-driven proposal generation, and EU-grade compliance into one workspace per client."*

### Future Market Outlook and Strategic Opportunities

#### Near-term (1–2 years)

- **AI-native bid platforms continue to consolidate.** Altura's Tendara acquisition is a template; expect 2–3 more EU consolidation events in 2026–2027. EU Solicit should evaluate strategic acquisitions in BG/CEE for instant data + customer base (Tender Service BG, GTS, or similar are candidates).
- **GovWin's 2026 AI proposal capabilities ship and prove the combined-segment thesis at US scale.** Expect EU buyer-side to start asking for an EU equivalent — increasing inbound interest for any vendor positioned correctly.
- **EC Procurement Data Space progresses but does not yet ship at production scale.** Window remains open for vendors to build proprietary value on top of fragmented data.
- **AutogenAI's UK Procurement Act 2024 success** prompts EU regulatory-act-native vendors. EU Solicit's admin-configurable compliance framework (RB v5 §6.7) is a directly-applicable wedge.

#### Medium-term (3–5 years)

- **Mature EU procurement-intelligence category emerges.** 3–5 dominant EU vendors with €30M–€100M ARR each. EU Solicit's path to being one of them is execution + sequencing, not feature invention.
- **Consultancy vertical specialisation deepens.** Vertical playbooks become product features (industry-specific scoring models, sector-specific content libraries). Moats deepen for vendors with EU consulting-firm density.
- **GovWin / Deltek EU entry via acquisition.** Most likely targets: Altura, Tendium, or a mature EU Solicit equivalent. **EU Solicit's optionality is to be acquired-or-entrenched by year 4.**

#### Long-term (5+ years)

- **AI agents are a layer, not a product.** The moat shifts to data, workflow, regulatory expertise, and customer-relationship density — exactly what EU Solicit's KraftData architecture and EU-compliance framework already point at.
- **Cross-segment platforms become the default** for consulting firms — single-segment specialists become niche tools.
- **The EU-Solicit-shape category** (intelligence + generation + grants + EU compliance) is, by year 5, **either a €50M+ ARR business EU Solicit owns, or a category Altura/GovWin/Deltek owns.** Year 1 execution determines which.

### Strategic Opportunities (Beyond the Core Bet)

1. **Acquisition-led BG/CEE consolidation** — Tender Service BG, GTS, and similar local players are <€1M ARR but have data + customer relationships that would take 18–24 months to build organically.
2. **Mercell partnership for e-submission integration** — closes the "self-service-only" gap from RB v5 without building portal connectors in-house.
3. **PNO Group strategic partnership / OEM** — PNO uses proprietary tools today. A white-label EU Solicit instance for PNO's 2,000+ clients would be a Series-A-defining customer relationship.
4. **Vertical SKUs for construction, IT, engineering, healthcare consultancies** — each with sector-tuned compliance frameworks, content libraries, and scoring models.
5. **Public-sector buyer module** (separate product, longer-term) — if EU Solicit's data corpus becomes credible, contracting authorities themselves become a customer segment, mirroring Mercell's two-sided model.
